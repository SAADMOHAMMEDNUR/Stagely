Budweiser Wassup

#1 35,000 votes

Budweiser's iconic 1999 "Whassup" commercial, created by DDB Chicago and inspired by Charles Stone III's short film "True," featured friends calling each other to shout the signature catchphrase while watching the game and drinking beer. The simple, repetitive, and relatable dialogue quickly became a global pop-culture phenomenon. Four friends call each other, asking "Whassup?!" in a loud, open-mouthed manner, followed by the response "Watching the game, having a Bud". Debuted during the 1999 NFL playoffs and the 2000 Super Bowl (XXXIV), becoming a massive cultural hit that led to major industry awards, including the Cannes Grand Prix.
