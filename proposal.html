Stagely Project Proposal: Empowering Fans to Shape the Super Bowl Halftime Show

Problem and Data Domain

The Super Bowl halftime show is one of the most widely viewed live music events in the world, drawing millions of viewers each year and generating significant cultural discussion both before and after the performance. In the months leading up to the event, fans frequently speculate about which artists might be selected, debating popularity, recent releases, cultural relevance, and past collaborations. These discussions often happen across fragmented platforms such as social media, forums, or comment sections, making it difficult for fans to see broader public opinion or compare artists in a structured way. As a result, individual opinions are scattered and quickly buried, and there is no clear way to explore or understand collective fan sentiment around potential halftime performers.

From an information perspective, this creates a problem around organizing and surfacing fan preferences. While many fans are interested in expressing their opinions and seeing how others feel, there is currently no centralized, user-friendly space dedicated specifically to predicting and discussing halftime show candidates. Existing platforms are not designed to aggregate votes, track artist popularity over time, or support meaningful comparison between artists. This lack of structure makes it harder for fans to engage deeply with the conversation and limits how public interest around potential performers can be explored and understood.

App Description

Stagely is the perfect app for fans of football and music! Evey year, at the biggest football game of the year - the Super Bowl - a Halftime Show takes place, where a major artist headlines the show. This is important for minority groups because if an underrepresented group gets to directly help an artist from their group headline the show, it increases representation. Stagely allows users to vote for their favorite artists to headline the Super Bowl Halftime Show. Users can create accounts, log in, and vote for their favorite artists from a list of nominees. The app will also provide information about each artist, including their background, music style, and previous performances. Additionally, users will be able to see the current vote counts for each artist and sort/filter by genre and popularity. Users can use our forum feature to make their case for why a certain artist(s) should headline the show.

Users will only be able to vote on artists whose managements contact Stagely. When users vote their favorite artists, they will be able to see how many votes that artist has received and where on Stagely's leaderboard they fall. When users are preparing to vote the artist, they can choose to additionallynominate guests to join the artist on stage.

Stagely solves our problem because oftentimes the NFL can be out of step of the current zeitgeist, which can result in poor ratings for the Halftime Show and even careers ending for the artist(s) responsible for the panned show. Stagely allows fans to directly influence the selection of artists who headline the show. This results in the Halftime Show feeling more in line with the current cultural moment.

App Features

Stagely will include the following features: An artist voting system where they choose to perform at the super bowl. Another feature would be like a guest artist that performs with the main artist. The last feature would be a community discussion forum where they can talk and discuss about artists and whatnot. Just being able to talk and give their opinions online about the main artist and the guest artist.

Information From An External Source

Users will be able to see artists details and live voter rankings for the Super Bowl Halftime Show. We will use a static CSV file to store and retrieve data.
